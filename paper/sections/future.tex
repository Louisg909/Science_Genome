\subsection{Limitations}
The framework has known structural limits that must remain visible in any use:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Semantic embeddings compress meaning and may omit methodological nuance.
    \item Citation graphs are incomplete proxies of intellectual dependence.
    \item Parent sets can be correlated, making attribution non-unique without constraints.
    \item Residual contribution can capture novelty, omission, or representation error.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Guardrails for responsible application}
To keep results inside scope and analytically valid, the following guardrails are mandatory:
\begin{itemize}
    \item report confidence intervals or stability summaries for inheritance estimates,
    \item separate descriptive findings from causal claims,
    \item retain audit trails for preprocessing and parameterization,
    \item and require expert interpretation for high-stakes conclusions.
\end{itemize}

These limits and guardrails are part of the final artifact and define the boundary conditions for publishable use.
