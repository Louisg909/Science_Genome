The framework is designed to support truth-oriented interpretation of scientific idea flow. Its central analytic distinction is between inherited semantic mass and residual contribution. This distinction is informative only when used with explicit interpretive guardrails.

\subsection{Interpretation policy}
A high contribution factor indicates lower explainability by cited parents within the chosen representation; it does not directly imply importance, correctness, or impact. A high inheritance factor indicates stronger semantic dependence on cited predecessors; it does not imply low value. Consolidation, synthesis, and benchmark-building can be highly valuable forms of scientific work.

\subsection{Permitted uses within scope}
Within project scope, outputs may be used to:
\begin{itemize}
    \item trace lineage structure for idea-flow hypotheses,
    \item compare inheritance and contribution patterns across branches or domains,
    \item identify candidate transfer pathways between streams,
    \item and support structured expert review of novelty claims.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Non-permitted interpretations}
The framework should not be used to make deterministic judgments about researcher quality, to claim causal intellectual influence without external evidence, or to replace expert reading and domain reasoning.

\subsection{Evidence standards}
Interpretations should combine quantitative outputs with uncertainty estimates, metadata context, and expert qualitative assessment. Strong claims require agreement across multiple indicators rather than dependence on a single score.
