\subsection{Inference}
For each target paper $i$, let $e_i \in \mathbb{R}^d$ denote its embedding and let
$P_i = [p_{i1}, \ldots, p_{im_i}] \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times m_i}$ collect the embeddings of
its cited parents.
We estimate inheritance weights $w_i \in \mathbb{R}^{m_i}$ by solving
\begin{equation}
\min_{w_i}\; \frac{1}{2}\lVert e_i - P_i w_i \rVert_2^2
+ \frac{\lambda_2}{2}\lVert w_i \rVert_2^2
\end{equation}
subject to either (a) non-negativity $w_i \ge 0$, or (b) simplex constraints
$w_i \ge 0$ and $\mathbf{1}^\top w_i = 1$.
Optionally, a sparsity budget keeps only the top-$k$ weights after each projected
update to improve interpretability.

The residual (novel contribution) vector is
\begin{equation}
r_i = e_i - P_i w_i,
\end{equation}
with reconstruction $\hat e_i = P_i w_i$.

We solve the constrained problem using projected gradient descent with deterministic
initialization.
At iteration $t$:
\begin{equation}
\tilde w_i^{(t+1)} = w_i^{(t)} - \eta\left(P_i^\top(P_i w_i^{(t)} - e_i) + \lambda_2 w_i^{(t)}\right),
\end{equation}
followed by projection onto the chosen feasible set (non-negative orthant or simplex),
and optional top-$k$ truncation.
The process stops when $\lVert w_i^{(t+1)} - w_i^{(t)} \rVert_2 \le \varepsilon$ or a maximum
iteration budget is reached.

For papers with no citations ($m_i=0$), we set $w_i=\varnothing$, $\hat e_i=0$, and
$r_i=e_i$.
